Oriki Archive
A community-driven, academically citable digital archive of Yoruba oral heritage. Built to preserve what is irreplaceable before it is lost.
Why this exists
Oriki — Yoruba praise poetry — is one of the most sophisticated forms of human cultural expression. A single family Oriki encodes genealogy, migration history, moral values, and spiritual identity. For over a thousand years it was transmitted orally from elder to child.

Today that transmission chain is breaking. Urbanisation, the diaspora, and generational disconnection have left millions of Yoruba people — especially outside Nigeria — unable to recite their own lineage poetry. When an elder who holds their family Oriki dies without passing it on, that knowledge is simply gone forever.

Oriki Archive exists to stop that loss. This platform was built by a diaspora Yoruba person who searched online for their own family Oriki and found nothing.

Two-tier trust system
The archive operates two tiers so it can serve both community contributors and academic researchers.
Community
All new submissions. Visible, searchable, and valuable. Can be endorsed, disputed, and nominated.
⭐ Verified
Entries reviewed against academic or primary sources by the advisory council. Permanently citable in academic work.
Content in the archive
Oriki — Yoruba praise poetry for families, towns, kingdoms, deities, occupations, warriors, hunters, and lineages.

Owe — Yoruba proverbs and wisdom sayings with literal translation and deeper cultural meaning.

Ijala — Hunters' praise poetry chanted to Ogun, the deity of iron and the hunt.

Dictionary — A community-built Yoruba word list with tone marks, parts of speech, and example usage.

Festivals — Documentation of Yoruba festivals with history, programme, and attendance planning for diaspora visitors.
Methodology and verification
Entries are never deleted. Disputes, corrections, and variants are all preserved as permanent record alongside the original submission. The community endorsement process — combined with review by academic advisors and cultural custodians — determines which entries reach Verified Corpus status.

For academic citation queries, community partnerships, or commissioning documentation of your family or royal lineage, contact: orikiarchive@gmail.com
Contribute to the archive
Do you know an Oriki — for your family, your town, a deity you honour? Submit it. A phone recording of an elder chanting is more valuable than a typed text. Every contribution, however small, helps preserve what is irreplaceable.
